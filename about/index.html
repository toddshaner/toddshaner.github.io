Insurance judgment, applied AI, and workflow realism.

I have spent my career in insurance, working across growth, advisory, and operating roles that required judgment, client understanding, and practical execution.

What interests me now is a specific question: how can frontier AI create real value in insurance without collapsing under workflow friction, weak implementation, or governance gaps?

That question matters because insurance is not a generic market. It is document-heavy, judgment-heavy, relationship-heavy, and regulated. The value of AI here will not come from abstract model performance alone. It will come from thoughtful application inside actual insurance workflows.

My focus

Practical AI in insurance

I focus on where AI can make insurance teams more effective: document handling, submission intake, research, knowledge retrieval, servicing support, renewal preparation, and internal workflow acceleration.

Operating model over hype

Most AI discussions overemphasize the model and underemphasize implementation. I am interested in what it takes to make these tools usable in practice: process design, role clarity, review gates, escalation paths, adoption, and measurement.

Governance in regulated environments

Insurance organizations need more than enthusiasm to deploy AI well. They need boundaries, auditability, privacy controls, review expectations, and clear ownership.

What I'm building

I am building a public body of work at the intersection of insurance, AI, and workflow design. That includes applied writing and analysis, prototype concepts and demos, governance and operating-model frameworks, and a GitHub portfolio focused on insurance-relevant AI use cases.

Why this site exists

This site is where I collect the work: writing, prototypes, frameworks, and case studies. The objective is straightforward: show how AI can be applied to insurance in a way that is commercially useful, operationally credible, and appropriate for regulated environments.

What I want to contribute to

  • Enterprise AI adoption in insurance
  • Brokerage and carrier workflow transformation
  • Frontier model use cases in regulated settings
  • Responsible AI governance and operating design
  • Product, strategy, and go-to-market work at the intersection of insurance and AI
